Investment basics in plain language

What are we calculating?

Investing is using money today in order to have more money in the future. This calculator helps you see what your final balance could be if you:

  • Start with a certain amount
  • Earn a steady yearly return
  • Add more money on a regular schedule

Key pieces

Return rate is how much your investment grows each year in percent. Starting amount is the savings you begin with. Investment length is how many years you leave the money invested. Additional contributions are extra amounts you add monthly or yearly.

What can this represent?

You can use this for many simple, long-term plans:

  • Regular deposits into an index fund
  • Monthly savings for a house deposit
  • Building an emergency fund or retirement pot

Things to keep in mind

Fixed return

We assume a constant return rate. Real investments like stocks or property move up and down over time, so actual results will vary.

Compounding

Compounding means interest is earned on top of previous interest. The more often it compounds (for example monthly instead of yearly), the faster the balance can grow.

Risk

Higher potential returns usually mean higher risk. This tool does not show risk – it only shows what could happen if the return rate stayed steady.

Fees & taxes

Management fees and taxes can reduce real returns. You can roughly include them by using a slightly lower return rate in the calculator.
