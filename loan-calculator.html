About each loan type

Amortized loan

The most common type of loan. You make regular payments, and each one reduces your balance a little. Includes home loans, car loans, and many personal loans.

Deferred payment loan

You don’t make payments during the loan. Everything is paid in one lump sum at the end. Used in some short-term business or balloon loans.

Bond

You receive money today and pay back a set amount later. The calculator shows how much you get now and how much of the difference is interest.

Loan basics

Interest rate

The cost of borrowing money. A higher rate means you pay more over time.

Compounding

How often interest is added to your balance. More frequent compounding = more interest paid.

Loan term

The length of the loan. Longer terms = smaller payments but higher total interest.

Secured vs. unsecured

Secured loans use collateral (like a car). Unsecured loans have no collateral and often higher interest rates.
